How we measure water availability

The Environment Agency split up the country into water bodies.

Surface water bodies are usually rivers and their tributaries, canals, lakes and reservoirs. Groundwater bodies lie underground and are called aquifers.

We measure availability at the local water body level using data from assessment points.

We will give you a water availability report for surface water and groundwater bodies in your local area.
