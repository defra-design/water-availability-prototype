Water availability for your area

The Environment Agency split the country up into areas called 'water bodies' in order to assess and regulate water. Each water body has a status indicating water availability.

Data from local assessment points is used to monitor water levels and make decisions about licencing and restrictions.

Surface and groundwater bodies

Surface water bodies cover a defined area of land around sections of rivers (and their tributaries), canals, lakes, reservoirs and coastal waters.

Groundwater bodies usually cover part of an aquifer - a natural underground layer of porous rock, sand or gravel that holds significant quantities of water.
