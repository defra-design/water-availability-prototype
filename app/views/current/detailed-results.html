Restrictions on abstraction

The Environment Agency monitor water levels for your area at your local assessment point.

All licences have restrictions on how much water you can abstract. There are also 'Hands-off Flow' (HoF) conditions which stop licence holders from taking water when the flow rate is critically low.

The current HoF for this assessment point is 135 Megalitres per day (Ml/d).

How Hands-off Flow is set

Hands-off Flow conditions are grouped into bands. As more and more water is licenced, the HoF level moves into the next band up and there is less water available to abstract.

HoF conditions in your area have reached band 4.

Band 1 has the most water available and no restrictions, while Band 6 is the most water stressed with no water available left for licensing.

Water availability in previous years

Water availability is usually better in winter because rainfall and water flow rates tend to be higher. In summer, water availability is often more restricted because it usually rains less which means flow rates are lower.
