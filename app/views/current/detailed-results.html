{% if (data['duration'] == "More than a year")%}

{% elseif (data['duration'] == "28 days or less") or (data['duration'] == "Less than a year") %}

If you only need water for a short period of time, instead of applying for a licence which can take a while to process, you may be able to enter into an agreement with an existing licence holder.

You can agree for them to give you part or all of their water abstraction rights permanently or temporarily. This is called the trading of water abstraction rights. Trades need approval by the Environment Agency to protect the environment and the rights of other lawful water users.

Find local abstractors to trade with using the map on DEFRA's Help for Water Rights Trading Data platform.

Applying for a licence

The Environment Agency grants water abstraction licences. When considering whether to grant a licence for your site, we consider other factors as well as groundwater and surface water availability.

Those other factors include the impact of the proposed abstraction on:

  • nearby abstractors
  • the natural environment

Changing your existing licence

If you have an existing water abstraction licence, you can apply to the Environmental Agency to make a change to your licence. This is called a variation.

Examples of variations include:

  • adding or changing an abstraction purpose (use)
  • adding or changing an abstraction point
  • increasing the volume of water to be abstracted, hourly, daily or annually
  • changing a gauging station or observation borehole referred to in a licence condition
  • changing the lining in an abstraction borehole referred to in a licence condition

To vary your licence, you will need to use the relevant water resource application forms and form guidance notes.
