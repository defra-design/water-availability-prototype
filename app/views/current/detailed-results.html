Common end date (licensing strategy review period)

The 'common end date' indicates when the licensing strategy for this area is due for review. The Environment Agency review each local licensing strategy every 12 years.

Most licences expire on the common end date but sometimes a shorter time limit is needed, usually due to local issues or uncertainty. If you want to abstract water after the expiry date, you must apply for a new a licence to replace the existing one.

You can apply for a longer licence period of up to 24 years but this will cost more and is granted at the discretion of the Environment Agency.

If the next common end date is over 6 years away, it will apply to your licence as the expiry date. If it is less than 6 years away, we will skip to the next common end date and apply that to your licence instead.

Maximum volume for abstraction

When you make your licence application you will need to estimate the amount of water you will use. Your licence will be granted with a restriction on maximum volume you are allowed to abstract. Guidance notes are provided with the application forms to help you estimate your predicted water usage.

Temporary restrictions (Hands-off Flow)

There are also 'Hands-off Flow' (HoF) conditions on licences. These come into effect when the flow rate is critically low.

When this happens you will be issued an alert and you are not allowed to abstract.

The current HoF for {% if (data['watertype'] == "ground")%} {{data.groundwaterCatchmentData[data.gid].properties.WB_NAME}} {% elseif (data['watertype'] == "surface")%} {{data.riverCatchmentData[data.sid].properties.WB_NAME}} {% endif %} is {% if data.sid == "1" %} 12 Megalitres per day. {% else %} 135 Megalitres per day. {% endif %}

How restricted is the water in your area?

Hands-off Flow conditions are grouped into bands and this gives you an idea of how water stressed your local water body is. As more and more water is licenced, the HoF level moves into the next band up and there is less water available to abstract.

Band 1 has the most water available and no restrictions, while band 6 is the most water stressed with no water available left for licensing.

Understanding groundwater availability

Water availability will depend on the location and depth of abstraction.

A pump test will be needed to determine availability for your specific abstraction point. You must get consent to investigate the groundwater source if you will abstract 20 cubic metres or more of water a day.

Regulations and restrictions

Restrictions may apply to licences where availability is limited or to protect the environment.

Hands-off Flow and Hands-off Levels are set by the Environment Agency as conditions on licences, these prevent abstractors from taking water when levels drop below a certain point.
