How are licencing decisions made?
The Environment Agency make licensing decisions based on strategy for your local area, which is reviewed every six years on the 'common end date'.

Common end date for {{data.riverCatchmentData[data.sid].properties.WB_NAME}}: January 2027

Licensing decisions will vary on a case-by-case basis depending on your chosen abstraction point, what you will use the water for, the number of other abstractors in the area and other factors such as environmental concerns.
How we monitor and protect water levels
The Environment Agency monitor water levels for your area at your local assessment point.

All licences have restrictions on how much water you can abstract. There are also 'Hands-off Flow' (HoF) conditions which stop licence holders from taking water when the flow rate is critically low.

HoFs are organised into bands. Each band has a certain amount of licences available until there is not enough water left at that band to sustain abstraction. At that point, the HoF for that assessment point drops down into the next band below, which will have tighter restrictions on how much water can be taken. In reality, this means more chance of days where you can't take water.
