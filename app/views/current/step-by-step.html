How to apply: step by step

Check what you need to do to apply for a water abstraction licence.

  1. Step 1: Check if you're in or impacting a designated site

    Some areas of the country require additional environmental protection, including those that have been designated as a protected area.

    The Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2017 (Habitats Regulations) provide a very high level of protection to 2 types of designated sites due to their special environment. These are:

    • Special Areas of Conservation (SAC), which contribute to biodiversity by maintaining and restoring habitats and species
    • Special Protection Area (SPA), which provides protection to birds and their nests, eggs and habitats

    Government policy treats Ramsar sites (internationally important wetland sites) in the same way as SACs and SPAs. Ramsars, SACs and SPAs are referred to collectively as European sites.

    You can use Natural England's maps and data to identify these protected areas and assess the likely impacts of your planned abstraction using the water body boundaries and environmental data.

    The Environment Agency are required to consult with Natural England when determining an abstraction or impounding licence within a site designated under either the Habitat's Regulations or Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 (W&CA).

    We may issue licences in these areas with stricter conditions or even refuse them to make sure these areas protected. For more information see types of protected sites and areas.

    If you complete any environmental assessment, you can submit this with your application as supporting information. You may need to hire a consultant or ecologist to help you do this. Please note that you may be asked for additional information or surveys when you apply if we are not satisfied that the risk to these species, habitats and/or sites have been fully considered.

  2. Step 2: Ensure you have rights of access

    ou must have rights of access to the water body at the point where you plan to abstract. This must be valid for at least 12 months from the start date of your licence (unless you only need a temporary licence for less than 12 months).

    We may ask you to provide proof of your rights of access. If you are the occupier, you may need to supply a map showing which land you occupy and the proposed abstraction locations. If you are not the occupier, you may be asked for a copy of the document giving you rights of access, such as a deed of grant, a conveyance, lease, licence or tenancy agreement.

    If you are not sure what rights of access or proof you need, get advice from the Environment Agency. We may have to return your application if you do not have the correct access rights or are unable to provide proof when we ask for it.

  3. Step 3: Check permits and other permissions needed

    As well as your abstraction licence, you might need other permits and permissions before you can abstract water.

    If you are planning to discharge water to surface water or groundwater, you will probably need an environmental permit. Getting an environmental permit can take up to 4 months, so it is essential that you apply soon if it is needed.

    If you are building a new obstruction on a river containing migratory fish or eels, you may also need to organise fish pass approval.

    Some licences also come with conditions on them to protect eels. You can read GOV.UK guidance on safe passage for eels, or contact the Environment Agency and ask to speak to your local fisheries team for advice.

    Make sure to check if you need a flood risk activity permit for carrying out any proposed works if they are in, or close, to a main river. For works on other watercourses (not on a main river), you should check if you need permission to do work on a river, flood defence or sea defence.

    Finally, you may need planning permission, for example if you are building storage. You can get guidance and apply for this by contacting your local council in your area.

  4. Step 4: Estimate the volume of water you will need

    Justification of need

    As part of your licence application, you will need to provide details of how you have worked out the amount of water you need to abstract.

    For example, you can give details of pump capacity by hours run, irrigation depth and number of hectares of crop requiring irrigation, specifications of equipment and processes, or industry recommendations and standards used.

    Tables with estimated volumes for some activities are available together with the water resources licence application forms.

    We will compare your expected water usage with benchmarks of good practice when making a decision on your licence application.

    Water efficiency

    You will also need to provide details of how you will be using water efficiently.

    For example, checking pipes for leaks, irrigating at night, using accurate irrigation techniques, checking soil moisture deficit, implementing a water management plan and auditing water consumption.

    We will compare this with good practice for your sector.

  5. and Check types of licences and charges

    Types of water resources licence

    There are different types of water resources licences you can apply for. They are:

    • a full abstraction licence - for most types of water abstraction over 20 cubic metres a day
    • a transfer abstraction licence - to move over 20 cubic metres of water a day from one source to another without intervening use
    • a temporary abstraction licence - to abstract more than 20 cubic metres of water a day over a period of less than 28 days
    • an impounding licence - to create or alter an impoundment structure such as a sluice, weir or dam

    If water levels in your area are usually low over the summer period, you may prefer to apply for a licence for only certain months of the year. Often abstractors will store water for use in the summer. In some cases, this can make a difference to whether your licence application is approved.

    Charges

    You must pay an application charge:

    • to apply for a new licence
    • for some changes (variations) to an existing licence

    You will also usually need to pay an annual charge (called a 'subsistence' charge) once you hold a full abstraction licence.

    Use the guidance on the charges for water resources licences to work out your application charge and annual charge.

    You may have to pay additional charges if the Environment Agency need to carry out extra work to determine whether you will be granted a licence. For example, completing conservation assessments or making a comparison between competing abstraction proposals.

  6. Step 5: Submit your application

    The Environment Agency grants water abstraction licences. When considering whether to grant a licence for your site, we consider other factors such as the flow rate for your local area, how much nearby abstractors are taking, the natural environment and any protected areas.

    Additional flow data might be needed to determine water availability in your area. You may be asked to submit flow data as part of your licence application. You can pay for an enhanced pre-application which will give you more information about whether this is needed and how to gather the data.
